WATER COLOR ART

Applied with a soft pointed brush or flat profile brush, watercolor is distinguished by the diversity of its strokes and the many ways that the dry and wet paint can be manipulated to form a broad array of effects.

PENCIL ART

Sketching is the process of making rapid, sloppy drawings that depict a subject's essential shape or outline.Creating tonal changes and the appearance of depth and texture through shading with pencils.

PEN ART

Pen art, also known as pen and ink drawing, is a type of visual art created on paper with various pens and ink. For centuries, pen art has been used to create everything from intricate illustrations to expressive sketches. The beauty of pen art lies in its adaptability, as artists can use a variety of pen types and techniques to achieve a variety of styles and effects.

TEXTURED ART

Textured art is an art form in which more emphasis is accorded to the physical quality of the surface. Unlike traditional paintings, which have their main focus on color and subject, texture art places its focus on the feel of the artwork. Through the use of different materials, such as plaster or acrylics, an artist often creates a three-dimensional effect extending from the canvas.

MANDALA ART

Mandala art is a spiritual and geometric design, most commonly associated with Buddhism and Hinduism, that represents the universe and wholeness. The word "mandala" itself means "circle" in Sanskrit.
